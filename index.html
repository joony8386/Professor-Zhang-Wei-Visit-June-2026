Hangzhou Survival Guide

Early June Weather

June marks the energetic transition into summer. The climate feels decidedly warm and humidity begins to climb. The dreaded "Plum Rain" season usually kicks off mid-month so you must prepare for sudden downpours!

  • Average High: 30°C (86°F)
  • Average Low: 23°C (73°F)
  • Pack an umbrella and light breathable clothing!

Local Culinary Delights

Hangzhou cuisine celebrates fresh ingredients and delicate flavors. It avoids heavy spices. You will discover subtle sweetness and refined textures.

  • West Lake Vinegar Fish: A legendary sweet and sour masterpiece made from fresh grass carp.
  • Dongpo Pork: Braised pork belly that literally melts in your mouth. It honors a famous ancient poet.
  • Longjing Shrimp: Fresh river shrimp stir-fried with fragrant Dragon Well tea leaves.
  • Beggar's Chicken: Tender chicken wrapped in lotus leaves and baked slowly in clay.

Must-Visit Attractions (Friday Tour)

Route 1: Classic Heritage

Ideal for first-time visitors seeking iconic landmarks.

  • West Lake: A UNESCO World Heritage masterpiece of classical Chinese garden design.
  • Lingyin Temple: One of China's wealthiest and most magnificent ancient Buddhist temples.
  • Feilai Peak: Limestone mountains covered in hundreds of historic Buddhist carvings.
  • Leifeng Pagoda: A legendary five-story tower offering panoramic views of the lake.

Route 2: Nature & Culture

Perfect for experiencing Hangzhou's renowned tea culture.

  • Guo Zhuang: A stunningly preserved classical private garden estate.
  • Meijiawu Tea Village: The lush green epicenter of Longjing (Dragon Well) tea production.
  • Yunqi Bamboo Forest: A serene walking path cutting through towering emerald bamboo.
  • Qinghefang Street: A bustling ancient pedestrian street perfect for souvenirs and snacks.
